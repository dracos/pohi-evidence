1.1. Post Office is the defendant in Alan Bates & Others v. Post Office Limited,
High Court of Justice Queen's Bench Division, Claim No. HQ16X01238 (the
“Claim’).

1.2 The Postmaster Litigation Steering Group (“PLSG”) is established to co-
ordinate Post Office’s approach and response to the Claim, and therefore
brings together representatives from affected teams in the business as set
out below at Table 1.

1.3. A deputy is permitted as a fully-empowered representative of the relevant
team, subject to this being agreed in advance with the PLSG Secretariat.

1.4 Other colleagues may be invited at the Chair's discretion.

1.5 The PLSG is chaired by the General Manager, Network, who has
responsibility for Network Operations. The Secretariat is provided by Post
Office Legal.

2. Terms of Reference

2.1. The objectives of the PLSG are to ensure that Post Office’s defence of the
Claim:

e protects the Network;

° is proportionately managed;

e does not place unplanned constraints or resource burdens on Post
Office; and

e is consistent with business as usual (BAU) practices, processes and
procedures.

2.2 In order to deliver these objectives, the Post Office Group Executive has
agreed the PLSG will undertake the following responsibilities:

a) providing a forum for cross-business discussion of Claim-related BAU
issues.so that all relevant matters are considered when making a
decision;

b) providing instructions to Post Office’s legal team on BAU/commercial
matters;

c) maintaining and progressing an Action Plan for Claim-related activity,
and monitoring the resources required to deliver against that plan;

d) signing off key Claim-related documents;

e) monitoring and approving Claim-related expenditure;

f) reviewing, challenging and signing off any Communications plans
developed as a consequence of the Claim’;

g) reporting matters to the Group Executive as appropriate.

2.3 Sign-off can be obtained through correspondence or discussion in a PLSG
meeting and is to be recorded by the Secretariat.

3. Escalation

3.1. Any issue which cannot be resolved at the PLSG should be escalated
immediately to the Post Office General Counsel by the Chair.

4. Organisation

4.1. The PLSG meets on an ad hoc basis as set by Post Office Legal. Any
papers for the PLSG are commissioned, co-ordinated and circulated by
Post Office Legal in advance.
