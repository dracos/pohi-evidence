Dear Laura,

I am seeking to appoint Nigel Railton as the interim Chair of Post Office for a term of 12
months. The priorities I have set for him include stabilising the organisation, intensifying
work to address Post Officeâ€™s historic failures and enabling the future success of the Post
Office through effective financial management and performance and delivery of critical IT
projects, and cost/headcount reductions at the centre to result in lower overall costs.

Nigel has strong executive experience and a good track record of crisis management, all
of which will be important in taking forward these priorities. Nigel will be taking up the Chair
role at a challenging time and I am keen that we do everything possible to support him in
delivering these objectives.

I expect that he will want to bring essential change to the Post Office to deliver on his
mandate. Government must support him on this. I am therefore keen that we agree to look
constructively at any requests he makes of Government with the aim of strengthening the
Post Office, and in principle agree that this may include the provision of funding if needed.

I am asking you to agree in advance a number of areas where we believe Nigel may ap-
proach us for support to ensure that we are collectively ready to make decisions at pace:

i.Funding to be made available for an incoming Chair to enable him to restructure
some key executive positions.

ii.Exemption from approval provisions on employee settlement agreements if re-
lating to such a restructure and to support the proposed exit of the Chief Finance
Officer.

iii.Potential for additional invest-to-save funding to be applied for within the current
Spending Review period.

iv.Funding flexibility, e.g. for Post Office Limited to be allowed to take out commer-
cial loans (or receive loans from HMG).
